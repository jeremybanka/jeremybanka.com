Subject Sans

a geometric–humanist fusion

Subject Sans’ architecture weds the helpful openness and character asymmetry of “warm” humanist structure to the mechanical purity of “cold” geometric constructivism. The cool neutral frames are casually proportioned and hung with subtle oblique contrast. The surface level is untouched for a perfect, digitally-cut appearance. The result is an approachable study in geometric postmodernism.

proportion

Half of Subject Sans’ body is given over to the x-space, with the remaining half divided evenly between the ascender and descender space. Capitals do not reach the full extent of the vertical space, but instead take up eleven twelfths, or eleven sixteenths of the entire body. This gives the ascender a full sixteenth alone.
